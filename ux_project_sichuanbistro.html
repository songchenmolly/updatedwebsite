Sichuan Bistro

H ouston is the largest metropolis in Texas, the third large city in United States. Unknown to the public, Houston’s Chinatown is also the largest number of East Asian restaurant in South United States. With generations of hard working, many of them have developing into national chains brand. Lack of communication with American mainstream of new technology, it is urgent for traditional Chinese in North American to introduce the modern restaurant information management system. Sichuan Bistro is one of them who trapped in the traditional management method.

Sichuan Bistro has more than 5 restaurants in Houston, and more than 20 restaurant located in Dallas, San Antonio, and the other cities. They found us to develop a system for online order. During the meeting he told us about several pain points.

    Literature Review: Sichuan Bistro Needed an Extra Staff Database

    Wrongful Labor Management

    I searched literature as my first step in designing the restaurant back-end system, since I was not familiar with the restaurant management field. Stated in the A Report: Chinese Restaurant in North America (YW Zhu, 2009), labor management (high population mobility) is the biggest profitable factor, even exceeding the chef departure and inflation.

    Suggested by the report, a shift management based on real-time sale could save amounts of money.

    However, in our company, the development service fee is based on the number of databases. A new database might cost a large sum of money. Luckily, with our project manager, he agreed to add the staff database after we presented our findings and plans to the manager.

    Role Design: An RBAC Model Based Staff Management

    RBAC Model

    Role-Based Access Control has four cores: User, Role, Permission, and Session

    Discussed with engineer and project manager, we decided to use the role-based access control modal to fit 1) role-based admin 2) different people might have multiple permissions, and 3) their expanding multiple business. At first, we needed to figure out what role/function they are playing in each functions.

    According to the pain points we dicussed in the first meeting, I draw an infographic to demonstrated users' permission to functions in different role.

    Users' Persmission Regarding to The Role

    Staff List Design: Location, Job, Role and a little special

    In Sichuan Bistro’s staff management, general manager could depend on the store location, job, and role to quickly find a certain staff. Meanwhile, saving staffs’ special information such like birthday or favorite food could enhance the relationship between restaurant and staffs.

    Dashboard Respective to the Role: Monitering, Glance, and Analysis

    The three functions of dashboard are: Monitoring Data, Glancing at the Important Data, and Checking the Analysis Report. However, how to decide the contents respective to the role in Sichuan Bistro dashboard?

    Scene analysis is a good way to find out the dashboard content, in which we used Usage Duration, Usage Frequency, Tasks, and Special Scene to help me to make the decision

    Security Design: Feedback, Details, and Role Access

    Feedback and Details

    Feedback with detailed information is important in back-end system, some critical actions could not recall or lead to bad result. Therefore, we used a lot of feedbacks with details information in critical actions such as deleting staff and dishes.

    Role Access and Communications

    Dashboard is customized to each users regarding to their role and job. However, in case of management error, a good communication is needed among users.

    Our company provided AI-assisted chatbox to help solve certain question, to some extent reduce the frustrations.
