Katy Myopia: Information Design

Kate Myopia Control Center is a 10-years Myopia Control Center served in Katy, Texas. Dr. Xu reflected that clients complained that they could not find the information they needed for the Orth-K (which is technology for control myopia). He wanted us to rebuild a website for the control center. Meanwhile he hopes this website can become the katy campaign of disseminating myopia control knowledge.

Who is the real users?

Parents of children with myopia

Do we need extra accessibility? The previous website providers charged a lot for extra accessibility website for vision disable group. Due to the limited budgets of developing fee, Dr. Xu provided the client’s demographic data: 1) All the ortho-K candidate are children between 5 to 16 years-old; 2) Nearly 90% clients who finding myopia control center is to browser ortho-k information. Finally, we decided not to develop vision disable websites. However, my color contrast and font size had passed the visibility test.

Website's Goal

Dissemination of Myopia Eduction

We categorized the information (refered on NIH websites) and used different presentation types:

The myopia information categories (Presentation Type): The basic knowledge of Myopia (Tree Structure), the branches of Myopia (Blog with Labels), daily care of Myopia （Newsletter and Landing News), and trends of Mypoia research (Newsletter and Landing News)

What's needed?

The process of fitting an Ortho-K

Fitting an Ortho-K needs a quite long time. During observing Dr. Xu's chat with parents, he spent most of time to explain the fitting process, since the process is too long, he had to go back again to explain the initial process. That's why a clear Ortho-K fitting process is needed to present in visual format.
